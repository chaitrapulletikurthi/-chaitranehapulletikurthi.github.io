How I think

Some people have opinions about dashboards.
I have opinions about decisions.

01

Define the metric before you mine the data

Most analytics projects fail before the first query. If you can't say what "better" looks like, no model will tell you. Before I open a notebook, I want to know what decision this analysis is supposed to change.

02

Signal is the exception, not the rule

Engineering taught me that noise is the default. I don't trust a pattern until I've stress-tested it, segmented it, and asked what would make it disappear.

03

AI should make decisions better, not just faster

Speed is the wrong benchmark. I build workflows where human judgment stays in the loop where it matters, and automation handles the parts where humans are consistently wrong.
